What is Pomodoro Technique?

The Pomodoro Technique is a way of managing your time in order to stay focused which was developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. It uses a timer to break work into intervals.

How Pomodoro Technique works?

The idea is to set a timer for 25 minutes and to work on a task until the timer goes off. If you get distracted or interrupted during the 25 minutes, start the 25 minutes of work over again. Once you’ve successfully focused on work for 25 minutes, take a 5 minute break. When your break is over, repeat the 25 minutes of work and 5 minute break. After you’ve completed four 25 minute blocks of work, take a longer 15-30 minute break.
